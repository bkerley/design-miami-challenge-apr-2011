Design Miami Challenge April 2011

VOTING IS AT bit.ly/dmcvote

The Design Miami Challenge for April 2011 is to design and build a website for a fictional Canadian-cuisine restaurant here in Coconut Grove. We will provide you with the menu, hours, and address, and you get to design and build the site. At the May meetup, you'll present your idea, we'll all vote on a winner, and they'll win a cool certificate and a prize of my choosing.

For mid-competition updates, follow FUN_DMC on Twitter.

This site is bit.ly/fun_dmc. Tell your friends!

If you just want the HAML/Markdown version of this site, view the source on GitHub.

Judging

The anonymous ballots for judging will include:

  • Correctness - does the information on your site match this page as it is during judging?
  • Accessibility - does your site work on mobile devices and with screen readers?
  • Architecture - is your site easy to navigate?
  • Theme - does your site match your perception of Canada?
  • Class - is your site classy?

Some Facts, Tips, and Guidelines

  • You don't have to keep my HTML; please alter it as you wish. Break it down, smash it to pieces, build it how you wish.
  • You don't have to use or not use any particular tools. Use what you like best.
  • People look at restuarant websites on both computers and phones.
  • Links to mapping/navigation services are nice to have.
  • This would be a good project to have in your portfolio.
  • Maybe you'll learn something too!

Restaurant Information

"The Grand Canadian Cafe" is a new fictional bistro in Coconut Grove featuring a selection of Canadian/American fusion cuisine celebrating the culture, heritage, and spirit of America's Neighbor to the North.

The restaurant is located at 2975 Oak Ave, Miami Florida, 33133 (this is actually Fire Station #8), is open for breakfast, lunch, and dinner; and has a bar open until midnight.

While many people think of the big eastern metropolis of Toronto when they think "Canada," also think of Calgary, home of the largest rodeo in the world; Banff, a resort town in the Rocky Mountains; and Vancouver, Canada's gateway to Asia. You might also think about Saskatchewan, which grows much of Canada's food; and the Northwest Territories, home of the TV series "Ice Road Truckers."
