Design Miami Challenge April 2011

The Design Miami Challenge for April 2011 is to design and build a website for a fictional Canadian-cuisine restaurant here in Coconut Grove. We will provide you with the menu, hours, and address, and you get to design and build the site. At the May meetup, you'll present your idea, we'll all vote on a winner, and they'll win a cool certificate and a prize of my choosing.

For mid-competition updates, follow FUN_DMC on Twitter.

This site is bit.ly/fun_dmc. Tell your friends!

If you just want the HAML/Markdown version of this site, view the source on GitHub.

Some Facts, Tips, and Guidelines

  • You don't have to keep my HTML; please alter it as you wish. Break it down, smash it to pieces, build it how you wish.
  • You don't have to use or not use any particular tools. Use what you like best.
  • People look at restuarant websites on both computers and phones.
  • Links to mapping/navigation services are nice to have.
  • This would be a good project to have in your portfolio.
  • Maybe you'll learn something too!

Restaurant Information

"The Grand Canadian Cafe" is a new fictional bistro in Coconut Grove featuring a selection of Canadian/American fusion cuisine celebrating the culture, heritage, and spirit of America's Neighbor to the North.

The restaurant is located at 2975 Oak Ave, Miami Florida, 33133 (this is actually Fire Station #8), is open for breakfast, lunch, and dinner; and has a bar open until midnight.

While many people think of the big eastern metropolis of Toronto when they think "Canada," also think of Calgary, home of the largest rodeo in the world; Banff, a resort town in the Rocky Mountains; and Vancouver, Canada's gateway to Asia. You might also think about Saskatchewan, which grows much of Canada's food; and the Northwest Territories, home of the TV series "Ice Road Truckers."

Menu

Have menu questions? Tweet them to FUN_DMC on Twitter so I can make decisions and update everyone on them.

Breakfast

  • The Vancouver: a whole-Saskatchwean-wheat bagel with Canadian cream cheese, fresh chives, and smoked Salmon from British Columbia.
  • The Calgary: a whole-Saskatchewan-wheat bagel with beef sausage, bacon, and eggs.
  • The Saskatoon: Belgian waffles with Saskatoon berry syrup.
  • Montreal Eggs Benedict: the classic, with Manitoban ham and garden-fresh chives.
  • The Lumberjack: Two pancakes with hearty Canadian maple syrup.
  • Paul Bunyan: Five pancakes with hearty Canadian maple syrup.

Sandwiches

All sandwiches are available on white, whole-wheat, or rye bread from Saskatchewan wheat. Sandwiches come with French fries, mashed potatoes, fresh vegetables, or steamed vegetables; this can be upgraded to a poutine for $1.00.

  • Quebecois Grilled Cheese: with optional horseradish.
  • Albertan Beef Burger: a quarter-pound all-beef patty with our special seasoning grilled just the way you like it.
  • Albertan Cheeseburger: featuring aged Canadian cheddar cheese.
  • Wanuskewin Buffalo Burger: a delicious and meaningful burger with Alberta-farmed buffalo.
  • Wanuskewin Buffalo Bacon Cheeseburger: with Canadian cheddar cheese and Manitoban bacon.
  • The Gimli Sliders: four miniature cheeseburgers that won't leave you hungry.
  • Red Deer BLT: made with Saskatchewan lettuce, greenhouse tomatoes, and Manitoba bacon.
  • Torontonian Club: sliced hard-boiled egg, Manitoba ham and bacon, lettuce, and brown mustard.

Entrees

  • Stampeder Steak: a 16 ounce top sirloin grilled over our very own "Calgary Flames," with an Alberta bean salad and bleu cheese mashed potatoes.
  • Hockey League Filet: an 8 ounce filet mignon wrapped with bacon, grilled to your standard, with potato wedges and asparagus.
  • Cookwagon Chicken: a grilled quarter chicken with steam-kissed green beans, carrots, and cauliflower.
  • Grandpa's Dry Ribs: made with a secret family recipe, these ribs are perfectly smoked and come with a green salad and potato wedges.
  • Salmon Victoria: oven-cooked salmon with a hint of maple and cinnamon, garnished with lemon zest, and served with bleu cheese mashed potatoes and asparagus.
  • The Ambassador of Poutines: a large poutine of French fries heaped with ham, cottage cheese, and gravy; with a green salad on the side.
